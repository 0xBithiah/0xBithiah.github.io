Education

Over the past few years, there's be a more positive light surrounding alternative education such as homeschooling and online schooling.

Now I personally attend an American online high school (TTU K-12) that's in fact run by Texas Tech University. Instead of going to a public or private secondary school like all of my friends in grade 7, I took a bizarre choice to do online schooling. Initially, it was so that I could pursue fencing competitively and travel around the world. Covid unforunately hit and everything crumbled (going abroad, etc.). But personally I found the American schooling system to be better than the Australian system and decided to stick with it.

Reasons

First of all, online schooling is different from homeschooling. I'm not a fan of homeschooling as that means your parents are your teachers...which kind of sucks for you and your parents (unless it's for some actual reason). I have actual Texas state approved teachers who have office hours and give lessons, just like a normal school but online.

[1] You get the whole American package: I'm talking about doing the SAT/ACT and PSAT. Participating in US high school only competitions in programming, etc. Did I tell you can do AP classes?

[2] Material you learn: Honestly, understanding American culture, history, etc. is very vital in understanding the 21st century itself. A huge part of global finance/politics happens in Washington, D.C.

[3] Teachers: I don't know what is so special about Texan teachers but they love what they do the most, teaching. You can feel the energy and effort in anything they teach.

[4] Flexibility: This is an understatement. Without the flexibility of online schooling, I wouldn't be able to accomplish what I have accomplished so far.

Can't list all of the advantages but the above are some of them. There isn't exactly anything wrong with the Australian education system but being physically in Australia, I can only attend a school physically. Unfortunately, Haileybury Pangea wasn't created in 2019. I might've chosen to attend Haileybury Pangea instead. But I take what first comes my way.

From a young age, I hate being constricted by something whether it be school or society. I loved (and still love) sports and I assume my rebellious energy came from it.

Options

Many have asked me whether there are online schools? There are several reputable ones such as (1) Texas Tech University K-12, (2) Standford Online High School, (3) UT High School, and if you're in Australia (specifically Victoria) you have Haileybury Pangea.

Is online schooling right for an individual? Most likely (95%) no. Just no. Everyone is different and I believe everyone should have education tailored to them. The beauty of online schooling is your whoe environment is tailored to you.
